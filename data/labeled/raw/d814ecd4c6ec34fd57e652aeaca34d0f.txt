    "text": "(CNN) -- Quoting Ezekiel 25:17 from \"Pulp Fiction\" will likely get you laughed out of Sunday school class.\n\nThat's because the Bible verse, as recited by Samuel L. Jackson's character, Jules Winnfield, doesn't actually exist.\n\nIn the film, Winnfield speaks these words to people before he kills them: \"The path of the righteous man is beset on all sides by the inequities of the selfish and the tyranny of evil men. Blessed is he who, in the name of charity and good will, shepherds the weak through the valley of the darkness. For he is truly his brother's keeper and the finder of lost children. And I will strike down upon thee with great vengeance and furious anger those who attempt to poison and destroy my brothers. And you will know I am the Lord when I lay my vengeance upon you.\"\n\nThe actual Bible verse says, \"I will carry out great vengeance on them and punish them in my wrath. Then they will know that I am the Lord, when I take vengeance on them.'\"\n\nScripture isn't the only thing writer-director Quentin Tarantino took creative liberties with for the film that premiered September 23, 1994. In honor of \"Pulp Fiction's\" 20th anniversary, here are 20 fun facts about the movie that still reigns as one of the greats:\n\nPotty breaks are bad news in this film. Whenever John Travolta's character, Vinnie Vega, takes a powder, tragedy occurs. During his three bathroom breaks, Mia Wallace (played by Uma Thurman) overdoses, the restaurant where he and Jules are dining is held up, and Bruce Willis' Butch Coolidge gets the drop on him.\n\nThe F-word is used 265 times in the movie. Go ahead and count it.\n\nFueled by the money he had made from his film \"Reservoir Dogs\" and the deal he had for \"Pulp Fiction,\" Tarantino took off to Amsterdam to write the latter. According to Vanity Fair, \"He bought school notebooks and declared about one of them, like a modern-day Hemingway, 'This is the notebook in which I am going to write Pulp Fiction.' \"\n\nHe ended up filling several notebooks.\n\nTarantino got a lot of use out of the Honda Civic that Butch drives in the film. It's the same car Pam Grier drives in the title role of \"Jackie Brown,\" and appears in a parking lot scene in \"Kill Bill: Volume 2.\"\n\nAt the beginning of the now-infamous overdose scene, Mia is wearing Vinnie's coat. Moments later she goes to light a cigarette and is no longer wearing it. The coat magically appears back on her once the cigarette is lit.\n\nRonnie Yeskel, the film's casting director, told Flavorwire that unlike on \"Reservoir Dogs,\" Hollywood agencies \"were pitching Robert Redford and Dustin Hoffman and Bruce, Bruce Willis -- just the biggest names in the business that you could imagine.\"\n\n\"And we're sitting there in these rooms with all these suits, and they all look alike, and we're like kids in a candy store,\" Yeskel said. \"We couldn't believe they were pitching these people to us.\"\n\nEllen DeGeneres tried out for the role of Jody, the pierced face wife of Vinnie's drug dealer, that ultimately went to Rosanna Arquette.\n\n8. Kathy Griffin made it in as did ...\n\nBlink and you may miss her, but comedian Kathy Griffin appears in the scene where bad guy Marsellus Wallace and Butch collide. Griffin has said she used to date Tarantino. Steve Buscemi, who now stars in \"Boardwalk Empire,\" also made a cameo as the Buddy Holly waiter at Jack Rabbit Slim's.\n\nThe scene where Travolta's Vinnie Vega plunges a needle into the chest of Thurman's Mia to revive her from an overdose using adrenaline was filmed with Travolta pulling the needle out of her and then the film was run backward.\n\nProducer Richard Gladstein told Vanity Fair that when Jackson did his second audition for his role he showed up with a burger and fries -- key props in one of the film's pivotal scenes. The actor proceeded to consume the meal so menacingly that Gladstein said, \"I thought that this guy was going to shoot a gun right through my head.\"\n\nOne of Tarantino's friends, a recovering heroin addict, advised Travolta on how best to portray a man riding that horse. He told the actor that getting drunk on tequila while relaxing in a hot bath would closely approximate a heroin high without him actually having to do the drug.\n\nThe director didn't have to look far for one of the film's most memorable props. Jules' wallet, which had \"Bad Mother F****r\" written on the front of it, actually belonged to Tarantino.\n\nWhen Captain Koons (Christopher Walken) tells a young Butch the story about the gold watch that was a family heirloom, he refers to a soldier named Winocki who transported it. Joe Winocki was the name of a character in the 1943 film \"Air Force,\" directed by Howard Hawks, one of Tarantino's personal heroes.\n\nNo one else could have played the man who specialized in messy cleanups: Tarantino reportedly wrote the character of Winston \"The Wolf\" Wolfe specifically for Harvey Keitel.\n\nIf Fabienne's line, \"Any time of day is a good time for pie,\" sounds familiar, it should. The character of Alabama says the same thing in another Tarantino film, \"True Romance.\"\n\nThere have been countless theories about what was in that mysterious briefcase that Jules and Vinnie had to protect -- with everything from gold to Marsellus' soul being suggested. But Tarantino has said in interviews that it's whatever fans choose it to be, though he has also had fun with fans promising a \"reveal.\"\n\n17. Keeping it in the family\n\nTravolta's character, Vinnie Vega, is the brother of Vic Vega, aka Mr. Blonde, from Tarantino's \"Reservoir Dogs.\" Michael Madsen played the character in that film.\n\n18. The meaning of the Band-Aid\n\nMarsellus sports a Band-Aid on the back of his neck for a reason: It covers up a scar actor Ving Rhames had.\n\nThe audience is led to believe that Vinnie and Mia won the dance contest at the Jack Rabbit Slim's restaurant. But late in the film, when Butch is returning to get his watch, there's a radio bulletin about the trophy having been stolen.\n\n\"Saturday Night Live\" alum Julia Sweeney had a bit role in \"Pulp Fiction\" as a junkyard owner and The Wolf's breakfast date, Raquel. Griffin has said she used to date Tarantino. Steve Buscemi, who now stars in \"Boardwalk Empire,\" also made a cameo as the Buddy Holly waiter at Jack Rabbit Slim's.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph24\"><strong>9. Tricky camera work</strong></p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph25\">The scene where Travolta's Vinnie Vega plunges a needle into the chest of Thurman's Mia to revive her from an overdose using adrenaline was filmed with Travolta pulling the needle out of her and then the film was run backward.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph26\"><strong>10. Jackson was convincing</strong></p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph27\">Producer Richard Gladstein told Vanity Fair that when Jackson did his second audition for his role he showed up with a burger and fries -- key props in one of the film's pivotal scenes. The actor proceeded to consume the meal so menacingly that Gladstein said, \"I thought that this guy was going to shoot a gun right through my head.\"</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph28\"><strong>11. On drugs</strong></p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph29\">One of Tarantino's friends, a recovering heroin addict, advised Travolta on how best to portray a man riding that horse. He told the actor that getting drunk on tequila while relaxing in a hot bath would closely approximate a heroin high without him actually having to do the drug.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph30\"><strong>12. That bad wallet</strong></p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph31\">The director didn't have to look far for one of the film's most memorable props. The watch transporter</strong></p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph33\">When Captain Koons (Christopher Walken) tells a young Butch the story about the gold watch that was a family heirloom, he refers to a soldier named Winocki who transported it. Joe Winocki was the name of a character in the 1943 film \"Air Force,\" directed by Howard Hawks, one of Tarantino's personal heroes.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph34\"><strong>14. \"The Wolf\"</strong></p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph35\">No one else could have played the man who specialized in messy cleanups: Tarantino reportedly wrote the character of Winston \"The Wolf\" Wolfe specifically for Harvey Keitel.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph36\"><strong>15. The same pie</strong></p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph37\">If Fabienne's line, \"Any time of day is a good time for pie,\" sounds familiar, it should. The character of Alabama says the same thing in another Tarantino film, \"True Romance.\"</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph38\"><strong>16. What's in the case?</strong></p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph39\">There have been <a href='http://www.cracked.com/article_19138_7-hotly-debated-movie-questions-that-totally-have-answers.html' target='_blank'>countless theories</a> about what was in that mysterious briefcase that Jules and Vinnie had to protect -- with everything from gold to Marsellus' soul being suggested. But Tarantino has said in interviews that it's whatever fans choose it to be, though he has also had fun with fans <a href='http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8_YIbC4dU_c' target='_blank'>promising a \"reveal.\" </a></p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph40\"><strong>17. Keeping it in the family</strong></p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph41\">Travolta's character, Vinnie Vega, is the brother of Vic Vega, aka Mr. Blonde, from Tarantino's \"Reservoir Dogs.\" Michael Madsen played the character in that film.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph42\"><strong>18. The meaning of the Band-</strong><strong>A</strong><strong>id</strong></p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph43\">Marsellus sports a Band-Aid on the back of his neck for a reason: It covers up a scar actor Ving Rhames had.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph44\"><strong>19. The dance trophy</strong></p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph45\">The audience is led to believe that Vinnie and Mia won the dance contest at the Jack Rabbit Slim's restaurant. But late in the film, when Butch is returning to get his watch, there's a radio bulletin about the trophy having been stolen.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph46\"><strong>20. The Gimp had a wife</strong></p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph47\">\"Saturday Night Live\" alum Julia Sweeney had a bit role in \"Pulp Fiction\" as a junkyard owner and The Wolf's breakfast date, Raquel. In real life, she had been married to Stephen Hibbert, <a href='http://www.npr.org/2014/05/24/315251305/bring-out-the-gimp-the-man-behind-the-mask-in-pulp-fiction' target='_blank'>who played The Gimp
