But we have prevailed.\"\n\nAnd India reached Mars with significantly less money.\n\nWith a price tag of $74 million, the Mars Orbiter Mission cost a mere fraction of the $671 million NASA spent on its MAVEN spacecraft, which arrived to Mars earlier this week. Some space observers noted that India's Mars orbiter cost less than the $100 million budget for the space thriller film \"Gravity.\"\n\n\"It shows how optimal is the design, that way we're able to cut cost and we're not compromising quality,\" said S. Satish, a space expert based in Bangalore.\n\nThe groundbreaking Mars mission wasn't without controversy -- with some critics who said India should spend the money on other issues.\n\nThe spacecraft launched on November 5, and has traveled over 650 million kilometers to enter Mars orbit. Its mission is to orbit the Red Planet, mapping its surface and studying the atmosphere. The Mars Orbiter kicked off its interplanetary debut with its own Twitter account.\n\nThe mission has been freighted with patriotic significance for India since its inception and is seen as a symbolic coup over its rival, China, which is also ramping up its space ambitions.\n\nChina's joint mission with Russia in 2011, which contained the Chinese Mars satellite Yinhuo-1, stalled and eventually fell back to Earth.
