Hours. How late each night and how many nights per week is the party good?\n\n2. People. Is it a good crowd -- cosmopolitan, friendly and, yes, good looking?\n\n3. Music. Judged on quality and variety offered in each city.\n\n4. Experience. How likely you are to have an experience that you'll still be telling your friends about a decade from now.\n\nI'm embarrassed to admit how much time I spent on this list (seriously, there was an Excel spreadsheet involved), but I finally narrowed it to 10 cities and I'm confident with the end result.\n\nFirst, my honorary mentions: Hong Kong, Sydney, Miami, Stockholm, Tokyo and Tel Aviv.\n\nMy apologies party people, but the competition was stiff.\n\nA lot of travelers continue to pass through Bangkok not realizing there's more to it than the seedy dregs of Soi Cowboy, or the backpacker haven of Khao San Road.\n\nBangkok has developed a vibrant and classy side as the city has become more cosmopolitan.\n\nWhether it's a series of gorgeous rooftop hotel bars downtown, rock bars near Ratchathewi, dance clubs just off Sukhumvit or trendy pubs in Thlonglor and Ekamai, there's legitimate, quality adventure to be had.\n\nBest place to mingle with the locals: The young kids prefer RCA, while many of the city's beautiful people are drawn to the clubs on Sukhumvit Soi 11 or upscale Ku De Ta, among others.\n\nMORE: Sleep is for wimps: World's 10 craziest party hostels\n\n\"The Paris of the South\" puts a New World spin on the insane Spanish practice of eating dinner at midnight and then dancing until dawn.\n\nWhile living there, I remember trying to go home as the sun came up and an Argentinian friend telling me she wanted to go to an after-party instead.\n\nIt was eight in the morning. On a Wednesday.\n\nAlthough not as affordable as it used to be, Buenos Aires is still a relative bargain among major world cities.\n\nBest itinerary: Start with the calmer bars of Recoleta and work your way up to the taverns of Palermo Hollywood. There's no rush, believe me.\n\nA staple. Get to this nightclub early or suffer through the two-hour line.\n\nMontreal is the undiscovered party gem of North America.\n\nBeautiful, friendly people, all night dancing to a wide variety of music.\n\nAnd, of course, the summer festivals.\n\nObvious tip, but still good to keep in mind: Don't go in winter.\n\nBest place to learn why the French do it better: Wood 35. Good drinks, good food.\n\nThe first rule of Barcelona is that you don't stay on Las Ramblas.\n\nThe second rule of Barcelona is that you don't stay on Las Ramblas.\n\nGuidebooks and pub crawls funnel the least savvy visitors there.\n\nOther parts of the city are far more interesting at night.\n\nClubs don't get going until well past 1 a.m. each day.\n\nMost of the top clubs are scattered throughout the northern part of the city.\n\nBest place to wake up early for: Row 14. Doesn't get going until 6 a.m.\n\nThe world epicenter of corner pubs touts some of the ritziest and most prestigious nightclubs in the world.\n\nYou can enjoy fantastic music almost any night of the week here.\n\nAnd there will always be the pubs.\n\nBest place to suffer local pretension: Chinawhite. Tell them you know a promoter named Becker even though you don't. You might get in.\n\nSao Paulo is the dark horse on this list.\n\nOften overshadowed by its more beautiful and glamorous sister, Rio de Janeiro, Sao Paulo is actually the cultural hub of Brazil.\n\nBut dig into the sprawling concrete mass and you'll find greater diversity and intensity on a nightly basis than almost any other place in the world.\n\nBest place to dance with a hot Brazilian until he/she has to go straight to work: D-Edge on a Thursday after 1 a.m.\n\nMORE: Insider Guide: Best of Rio de Janeiro\n\nAlthough it's not true that the \"city never sleeps\" (it's usually done by 3 or 4 a.m.), New York City scores big simply because it offers something for everybody on any night of the week.\n\nWarehouse parties in Brooklyn, mega-clubs in Meatpacking, trendy hipster joints in the East Village, hotel lounges, college bars, indie rock shows, loft parties, hip hop and jazz, heavy metal and opera -- there's almost no limit to the ways this city entertains you.\n\nBest place to buy a single drink for more than you spend on some nights out: Meatpacking District. Bring a credit card.\n\nIf a city's mental health was determined by how many hours of the week its clubs weren't active, Berlin would be a lunatic in a straightjacket, repeatedly slamming its head against a padded wall.\n\nWitness: walking out of a venue at 9 a.m. on a Sunday to find that there's still a line to get in; a club with beds installed on its bottom floor so patrons can sleep without the inconvenience of having to leave; ceilings made completely of LED lights; a swimming pool next to the dance floor; bouncers with face tattoos.\n\nBest place(s) to experience the big-club vibe: Berghaim. If you can't get in (it's random), then Watergate.\n\nEvery party hound must make a pilgrimage to Ibiza at least once.\n\nThe venues aren't as much clubs as they are life experiences.\n\nNowhere else have I seen a wind tunnel built into a dance floor or parade floats and foam dropped from the ceiling like it's the Fourth of July indoors.\n\nIn Ibiza, you do beach parties during the day, hotel bars in the evening and dance to the best DJs in the world all night.\n\nBest place to have an out-of-body techno experience: The clubs in Ibiza Town (rather than San Antonio) are worth the extra expense.\n\nTell us about your own favorite party cities in the comments.\n\nMark Manson is an author and entrepreneur.",
