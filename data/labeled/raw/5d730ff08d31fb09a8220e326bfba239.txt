    "text": "Venice, Italy (CNN) -- Public transport in Venice is like an endless sightseeing tour, and not only for the amount of tourists on the central vaporetto routes.\n\nThe waterfront architecture is truly spectacular, from Palladian churches to private palazzos.\n\nAnd now during the Biennale contemporary artworks and installations are scattered around the city.\n\nVenturing along the waterways leads to some unmissable exhibitions, where art and architecture blend into a beautiful dialogue.\n\nClose to the Rialto market on the Canal Grande, an 18th century palazzo is home to Fondazione Prada, where all three floors are open to the public for the first time.\n\nDuring the Biennale it's hosting a show curated by Germano Celant, one of the leading figures of the 1960s anti-formal art movement Arte Povera.\n\nCelant has filled the rooms with a theatrical show.\n\nRead: Sculptures made from nothing but plain paper\n\nFrom 16th century musical instruments, clocks and mechanical curiosities to modern installation pieces involving performers, Art or Sound explores both formal and conceptual ideas relating to music.\n\nYou can witness the craft of historic pieces as well as works by key avant-garde artists from Celant's previous collaborations, such as John Cage, Nam June Paik, Michelangelo Pistoletto and Jannis Kounellis.\n\nTheir radical rethinking of art and the abstract condition of sound resonates throughout the exhibition.\n\nThe unique Venetian Baroque interiors add another level of interest to the show.\n\nIt's both raw and historical - unlike any other contemporary art space in the world - with ornaments, carvings and rich details.\n\nThe Prada Foundation has a contract as a patron to rescue the building and bring the original frescoes and rare, fine materials back to life, \"without trying to modify it through characterless facelifts\", Celant explains.\n\nOn the other side of the water, after the Rialto Bridge, there is the Fondaco Marcello, an exhibition space in a 15th century arms storehouse by the water front.\n\nHere the Biennale theme of Modernity extends into a scientific narrative, looking at possible ways of existence on the Antarctic continent, a subject in total contrast to the surrounding architectural heritage in Venice.\n\nThe Antarctic Pavilion, curated by Nadim Samman makes you think of lifestyle and tourism, as well as the ecological and political questions posed by those extreme conditions on the margins of civilization.\n\nFurther down towards San Marco Square, a giant composition of 1,179 stainless steel bikes has been neatly stacked on the canal side courtyard garden of the Palazzo Cavalli-Franchetti near the Accademia bridge.\n\nThis art installation, titled \"Forever\" by Ai Weiwei is part of the exhibition Genius Loci, brought to Venice by the Lisson Gallery.\n\nThe show continues inside the 16th century palace building with sculptures and installations by international artists including Tony Cragg, Lee Ufan, Julian Opie and Joana Vasconcelos.\n\nThe rest is slightly more modest in scale than Ai Weiwei's installation, but still spectacular examples of ideas addressed in contemporary public works.\n\nThere is a sense of play with architecture and space, exploring the parallels between the two disciplines, such as Anish Kapoor's work which deals with our spatial perceptions.\n\nThis exhibition suggests that genius loci 'the special spirit' can be found in the urban space through artworks, materials and forms; a departure from the modernist obsession with functionality.\n\nA short hop on a vaporetto takes you across the lagoon from San Marco to the island of San Giorgio Maggiore, which has become a site for displaying large public installations.\n\nThis year a geometric sculpture entitled \"The Sky Over Nine Columns\" dominates the waterfront square next to Palladio's famous church.\n\nIts surfaces are covered with golden glass mosaics, beautifully reflecting the Venetian sun.\n\nThe piece by Heinz Mack, who represented Germany at the Arts Biennale in 1970, was commissioned by the Cini Foundation. Mack is known for monumental installations: \"My sculptures are objects of light in space\" Mack said.\n\nRead: Is biology the future of design?\n\nBehind the church, an exquisitely crafted pavilion has been erected in the garden of the Stanze del Vetro glass museum.\n\nThe aquarium-like Glass Tea House Mondrian is designed by Japanese artist Hiroshi Sugimoto, best known for his minimalistic photography.\n\nSugimoto's pavilion is the first in a series of commissions, supported by the Cini Foundation, which follows the model of the Serpentine Pavilion in London. Installed inside a cedar wood fenced garden, the glass pavilion will also be the setting for a traditional tea ceremony, a performance which exemplifies the delicacy in Japanese culture.\n\nAll these shows run parallel to the Architecture Biennale, overseen by Dutch architect Rem Koolhaas this year.\n\nEven if you don't have time to visit the Giardini and the national pavilions, these events outside the main programme are some fantastic cultural offerings, adding another level of meaning to visiting a city with such rich heritage.",

sightseeing tour, and not only for the amount of tourists on the central vaporetto routes.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph2\">The waterfront architecture is truly spectacular, from Palladian churches to private palazzos.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph3\">And now <a href='http://edition.cnn.com/2014/06/16/travel/venice-architecture-biennale-rem-koolhaas/'>during the Biennale</a> contemporary artworks and installations are scattered around the city.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph4\">Venturing along the waterways leads to some unmissable exhibitions, where art and architecture blend into a beautiful dialogue.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph5\"><strong>Baroque influence </strong></p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph6\">Close to the Rialto market on the Canal Grande, an 18th century palazzo is home to <a href='http://www.fondazioneprada.org/' target='_blank'>Fondazione Prada</a>, where all three floors are open to the public for the first time.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph7\">During the Biennale it's hosting a show curated by <a href='http://www.guggenheim.org/index.php?option=com_content&amp;view=article&amp;id=27&amp;Itemid=246' target='_blank'>Germano Celant</a>, one of the leading figures of the 1960s anti-formal art movement Arte Povera.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph8\">Celant has filled the rooms with a theatrical show.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph9\"><a href='http://www.cnn.com/2014/08/29/world/astonishing-sculptures-made-from-nothing-but-paper/index.html' target='_blank'>Read: Sculptures made from nothing but plain paper </a></p>\n<div class=\"cnn_strylftcntnt\"><div class=\"cnn_strylctcntr cnn_strylccimg300\">\n<div class=\"cnn_strylccimg300cntr\">\n<img src=\"http://i2.cdn.turner.com/cnn/dam/assets/140903132222-venice-biennale-palazzo-story-body.jpg\" alt=\"This 18th century palazzo houses the Prada Foundation \" border=\"0\" class=\"box-image\" height=\"169\" width=\"300\"/>\n</div>\n<div>This 18th century palazzo houses the Prada Foundation </div>\n</div></div>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph10\">From 16th century musical instruments, clocks and mechanical curiosities to modern installation pieces involving performers, Art or Sound explores both formal and conceptual ideas relating to music.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph11\">You can witness the craft of historic pieces as well as works by key avant-garde artists from Celant's previous collaborations, such as John Cage, Nam June Paik, Michelangelo Pistoletto and Jannis Kounellis.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph12\">Their radical rethinking of art and the abstract condition of sound resonates throughout the exhibition.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph13\">The unique Venetian Baroque interiors add another level of interest to the show.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph14\">It's both raw and historical - unlike any other contemporary art space in the world - with ornaments, carvings and rich details.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph15\">The <a href='http://www.fondazioneprada.org/' target='_blank'>Prada Foundation</a> has a contract as a patron to rescue the building and bring the original frescoes and rare, fine materials back to life, \"without trying to modify it through characterless facelifts\", Celant explains.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph16\"><strong>Modernity takes over</strong><strong> </strong><strong> </strong></p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph17\">On the other side of the water, after the Rialto Bridge, there is the Fondaco Marcello, an exhibition space in a 15th century arms storehouse by the water front.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph18\">Here the Biennale theme of Modernity extends into a scientific narrative, looking at possible ways of existence on the Antarctic continent, a subject in total contrast to the surrounding architectural heritage in Venice.</p>\n<div class=\"cnn_strylftcntnt\"><div class=\"cnn_strylctcntr cnn_strylccimg300\">\n<div class=\"cnn_strylccimg300cntr\">\n<img src=\"http://i2.cdn.turner.com/cnn/dam/assets/140826165354-venice-biennale-ai-weiwei-story-body.jpg\" alt=\"Ai Weiwei exhibits a stack of 1,179 stainless steel bikes \" border=\"0\" class=\"box-image\" height=\"169\" width=\"300\"/>\n</div>\n<div>Ai Weiwei exhibits a stack of 1,179 stainless steel bikes </div>\n</div></div>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph19\">The Antarctic Pavilion, curated by <a href='http://nadimsamman.com/' target='_blank'>Nadim Samman</a> makes you think of lifestyle and tourism, as well as the ecological and political questions posed by those extreme conditions on the margins of civilization.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph20\">Further down towards San Marco Square, a giant composition of 1,179 stainless steel bikes has been neatly stacked on the canal side courtyard garden of the <a href='Palazzo Cavalli-Franchetti' target='_blank'>Palazzo Cavalli-Franchetti</a> near the Accademia bridge.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph21\"><a href='http://www.cnn.com/2014/07/23/travel/worlds-coolest-bookstores/index.html' target='_blank'>Read: World's coolest bookstores</a></p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph22\">This art installation, titled \"Forever\" by Ai Weiwei is part of the exhibition Genius Loci, brought to Venice by the <a href='http://www.lissongallery.com/' target='_blank'>Lisson Gallery</a>.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph23\">The show continues inside the 16th century palace building with sculptures and installations by international artists including Tony Cragg, Lee Ufan, Julian Opie and Joana Vasconcelos.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph24\"><strong>Contemporary public art</strong></p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph25\">The rest is slightly more modest in scale than Ai Weiwei's installation, but still spectacular examples of ideas addressed in contemporary public works.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph26\">There is a sense of play with architecture and space, exploring the parallels between the two disciplines, such as Anish Kapoor's work which deals with our spatial perceptions.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph27\">This exhibition suggests that genius loci 'the special spirit' can be found in the urban space through artworks, materials and forms; a departure from the modernist obsession with functionality.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph28\">A short hop on a vaporetto takes you across the lagoon from San Marco to the island of San Giorgio Maggiore, which has become a site for displaying large public installations.</p>\n<div class=\"cnn_strylftcntnt\"><div class=\"cnn_strylctcntr cnn_strylccimg300\">\n<div class=\"cnn_strylccimg300cntr\">\n<img src=\"http://i2.cdn.turner.com/cnn/dam/assets/140903124017-san-giorgio-maggiore-island-venice-biennale-story-body.jpg\" alt=\"The beautiful island of San Giorgio Maggiore\" border=\"0\" class=\"box-image\" height=\"169\" width=\"300\"/>\n</div>\n<div>The beautiful island of San Giorgio Maggiore</div>\n</div></div>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph29\">This year a geometric sculpture entitled \"The Sky Over Nine Columns\" dominates the waterfront square next to Palladio's famous church.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph30\">Its surfaces are covered with golden glass mosaics, beautifully reflecting the Venetian sun.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph31\">The piece by Heinz Mack, who represented Germany at the Arts Biennale in 1970, was commissioned by the <a href='http://www.cini.it/en/foundation' target='_blank'>Cini Foundation</a>. Mack is known for monumental installations: \"My sculptures are objects of light in space\" Mack said.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph32\"><a href='http://www.cnn.com/2014/08/27/tech/innovation/biodesign-why-the-future-of-our-cities-is-soft-and-hairy/index.html' target='_blank'>Read: Is biology the future of design? </a></p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph33\">Behind the church, an exquisitely crafted pavilion has been erected in the garden of the Stanze del Vetro glass museum.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph34\">The aquarium-like Glass Tea House Mondrian is designed by Japanese artist Hiroshi Sugimoto, best known for his minimalistic photography.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph35\">Sugimoto's pavilion is the first in a series of commissions, supported by the Cini Foundation, which follows the model of the Serpentine Pavilion in London. Installed inside a cedar wood fenced garden, the glass pavilion will also be the setting for a traditional tea ceremony, a performance which exemplifies the delicacy in Japanese culture.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph36\">All these shows run parallel to the Architecture Biennale, overseen by Dutch architect Rem Koolhaas this year.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph37\">Even if you don't have time to visit the Giardini and the national pavilions, these events outside the main programme are some fantastic cultural offerings, adding another level of meaning to visiting a city with such rich heritage.</p>\n<p class=\"cnn_storypgraphtxt cnn_storypgraph39\"><i><a href='http://www.labiennale.org/en/architecture/' target='_blank'>Venice Architecture Biennale</a></i><i> 2014: 7.6 - 23.11.2014</i></p>\n<script type=\"text/javascript\">if(typeof CNN.expElements==='object'){CNN.expElements.init();}</script>\n<p class=\"cnn_strycbftrtxt\"></p>\n<!--endclickprintinclude--><!--google_ad_section_end-->\n<p class=\"cnn_strycbftrtxt\">&copy; 2012 <a href=\"http://www.grandtourmagazine.com/ \" target=\"_blank\">Grand Tour Magazine</a>.
